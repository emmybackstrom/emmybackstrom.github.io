PROBLEM FRAMING IDEATION DEVELOPMENT

29K

App feature pitch for hackathon

CHALLENGE

29k is a mental health app with courses, challenges, meditations and other content meant to facilitate personal development. During my internship at 29k, there was an org-wide hackathon in which everyone had 2 days to come up with, develop and pitch an idea which would support 29k’s mission.

Something which I had previously identified as a problem through UX research was that new users experience a lack of guidance and aren’t sure what to do or how to work towards a goal after creating an account. This in turn leads to a low retention rate, which is why I chose to focus on how to solve this problem.

TEAM

  • Strategy & Design - Emmy Bäckström

PROBLEM FRAMING

Though I started with a problem in mind, it wasn’t specific enough and clearly defined. More importantly, I wanted to challenge my problem by asking a set of questions to be sure it was a relevant problem to solve. Therefore, I went through the process of problem framing in order to have one clear problem statement for ideation.

Using a workshop structure from Design Sprint Academy, I identified what type of user is affected by the problem, what their experience is like when they are, in what contexts they’re affected and finally connected it to both user and business values. I ended up with the problem statement “People who are new to actively working on personal development lack guidance when planning activities and creating habits which support personal growth. We need to provide guidance for defining what areas they want to develop in, and how to go about it.”.

IDEATION

When moving from problem framing to ideation, I used my problem statement to formulate a concise focus question: “How might we make users feel encouraged to use the app?”. I used the same ideation process as in a previous sprint I had done, and therefore went on to rapid research about my topic for 15 minutes to get ideas of what other companies are doing to address this.

After getting inspiration, I came up with 4 concepts: Personalized plan, Rewarding illustration and copy after activity, Streaks and Badges. After asking my team members for feedback, and learning from our psychologists why gamification features like streaks and badges aren’t effective in contexts which involve deep focus and effort, I decided to develop the first two ideas.

DEVELOPMENT

For the personalized plan, I envisioned that as a final stage in onboarding the user would get the chance to answer two simple questions about their intention with using the app and based from that get a growth plan. This plan would contain a daily, weekly and monthly overview of curated exercises relating to what the user answered that they were interested in. The plan helps tackle many of the problems I identified during the first stage, like providing guidance on what to do in order to reach goals, staying motivated by being able to view progress etc.

The rewarding illustration and copy was a one screen design where I aimed to improve the final screen of doing an activity in the app. I implemented a progress bar which shows a users overall progress on their personal growth plan, and illustrated a character which provides a more unique and original impression than a stock photo.

”In this project, after only spending a handfull of days with our team, Emmy has identified an area in our app that multiple user interviews and data analysis confirmed as crucial for user engagement and retention. By asking users two simple questions very early in their journey, we would not only be able to tailor what we offer to individual user’s needs, but also get to know our users better, in general. Unlike an ordinary survey, users’ motivation to answer those questions would be their own benefit, and the questions would feel inspiring and validating rather than intrusive. Overall, we’ve been hugely impressed by the cleverness, elegance and neat design of this project, even without considering the short time they were done in and the very limited introduction Emmy has had into our product and UX research around it. It fits so perfectly into what the app already has, while adding huge value with small means.”
- Sonja Petrović Lundberg, CPO
