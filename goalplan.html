RESEARCH IDEATION DEVELOPMENT

GOALPLAN

Redesigning the brand of a sales performance platform company.

CHALLENGE

Goalplan is a software company helping large, complex, and distributed sales organizations improve their communication, processes, and performance.

As they’re growing from a start-up to a scale-up, Goalplan feels a need to establish a strong brand which visually attracts the various C-Level executives in their targeted industries (retail, MLM, retail banking, and telecom) across multiple continents as well as appealing to the end-users of their application. Goalplan also expressed the importance of having a unique identity which stands out among a sea of similar-looking corporate identities.

TEAM

  • Visual Designer - Emmy Bäckström
  • 3D & Logo Designer- Larissa Poderoso
  • Copywriter - Axel Ahlgren
  • Project Manager - Veronica Wilbacher

RESEARCH

In order for us to get a solid understanding of what exactly wasn’t effective about their current visual identity, we analyzed all of their channels and platforms and performed a visual audit in which we compared Goalplan to similar brands. Insights that came up concerned the lack of uniqueness (using a generic logo, generic website template and stock photos) and that most other brands similar to Goalplan had a serious identity using typical corporate blue as their primary color.

This gave us the first springboard in designing new visuals, but what was also important before moving on was getting a strong strategic foundation in place. In initial meetings, it became clear that the Goalplan team didn’t share the same perspective on the mission, vision and personality of Goalplan. Therefore, we facilitated a workshop designed to get them aligned on strategy so that we would ideate in the right direction in the following stage of the process.

IDEATION

After research, it was clear that the strategic foundation would be based on the brand being friendly, approachable and modern in contrast to the more serious competitors. This will help Goalplan be unique and have a brand that is not only attractive to C-level executives but also by frontliners.

With that in mind, we explored the visual direction through stylescapes. We created different stylescapes with various typography, colors, imagery and iconography style and presented this to the client. After picking and mixing the best bits of each stylescape, we moved on to creating the comprehensive brand guideline and additional assets.

DEVELOPMENT

The brand guideline is based on the strategic foundation we set in the beginning of the project, and contains instructions on using assets such as logotype, illustrations and icons.
